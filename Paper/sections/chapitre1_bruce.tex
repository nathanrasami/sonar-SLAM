\section{Bruce-SLAM adapted to Aracati2017}
\label{sec:chap1}

This chapter describes the \emph{base} method: the Bruce-SLAM system~\hyperref[bib:bruceslam]{[1]} made
operational, GT-free, on Aracati2017. The original architecture is kept unchanged;
the following sections describe what had to be \textbf{added} for it to work —
on-board odometry and USBL anchoring — then the results under the protocol of
\autoref{sec:protocole}.

\subsection{Bruce-SLAM pipeline recap}

The front-end extracts ``structure'' points from each sonar image with a
\textbf{SOCA-CFAR} detector; successive scans are aggregated into \textbf{keyframes}
(created beyond a displacement threshold) and registered by sequential \textbf{ICP}
initialised with the odometry. The back-end is a factor graph optimised with
\textbf{iSAM2}~\hyperref[bib:isam2]{[9]}. ``Non-sequential'' loop closures (NSSM) are detected
geometrically: preselection by covariance and estimated overlap, global ICP
initialisation with the \textbf{shgo} optimiser, validation by pairwise consistency
(\textbf{PCM}~\hyperref[bib:pcm]{[6]}). An SSM module (short-term multi-frame registration) refines the
odometry.

\subsection{GT-free odometry: integrating \texttt{/cmd\_vel}}
\label{sec:odom}

Aracati2017 provides no usable inertial odometry; we integrate the on-board velocity
commands (planar unicycle model):
\begin{equation}
\theta_{k+1} = \theta_k + \omega_{z,k}\,\Delta t_k, \qquad
\mathbf{p}_{k+1} = \mathbf{p}_k + v_{x,k}\,\Delta t_k\,[\cos\theta_k,\ \sin\theta_k]^\top
\end{equation}
with the initial state $(\mathbf{p}_0, \theta_0)$ estimated from the
course-over-ground of the first USBL fixes (no ground truth). Since $\omega_z$ is
derived from the compass~\hyperref[bib:aracati]{[8]}, the integrated \textbf{heading} is accurate (relative
rotation error $\approx 0$~°/m) but the \textbf{translation} drifts strongly
(commands $\neq$ actual velocities; currents): \textbf{19.6--21.3 m} of start-pinned
ATE over the mission (orange curve in \autoref{fig:bruce-err} and
\autoref{fig:bsu-err}). The whole point of the back-end is to correct this translation
without touching the heading.

\subsection{USBL anchoring in the factor graph}
\label{sec:usbl}

The USBL provides noisy absolute position fixes $\mathbf{u}_i$ (multipath, outlier
pings $\sim$73 m off near the transponder). In the code, the USBL can act at three
places, and one must be precise about what is actually enabled:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{frame initialisation} (front-end, once): position of the first fix +
  heading from the course-over-ground of the first fixes — this is the seed
  $(\mathbf{p}_0, \theta_0)$ of \autoref{sec:odom};
\item \textbf{continuous dead-reckoning correction} (front-end): a causal
  complementary filter pulls the integrated position towards each accepted fix,
  $\mathbf{p} \mathrel{+}= g\,(\mathbf{u}_i - \mathbf{p})$ (best standalone gain
  $g = 0.4$, measured offline) — \emph{disabled everywhere} in this document;
\item \textbf{position factors in the graph} (back-end): the retained mechanism,
  described below.
\end{itemize}
Enabling both the front-end correction and the back-end factors is a contradictory
double anchoring: the already-corrected odometry fights the factors, with a measured
$\times 3$ ATE degradation. All configurations in this document therefore use
\textbf{seed + back-end only}.

Each fix is associated with the closest keyframe in time ($|t_i - t_k| \le 1$ s,
3 m/s speed gating) and added as a \textbf{robust unary factor on position only}
(the heading is left to the odometry and the loops):
\begin{equation}
\phi_i(\mathbf{X}) = \rho_c\!\left(\frac{\lVert \Pi\,\mathbf{X}_{k(i)} - \mathbf{u}_i \rVert}{\sigma_{\text{usbl}}}\right),
\qquad \rho_c(r) = \tfrac{c^2}{2}\,\log\!\left(1 + \tfrac{r^2}{c^2}\right)
\end{equation}
where $\Pi$ projects the pose onto $(x, y)$ and $\rho_c$ is the Cauchy kernel
(outliers saturate instead of pulling the solution).

\subsection{Results — Bruce-SLAM alone (start-pinned protocol, \autoref{sec:protocole})}
\label{sec:chap1-res}

Two runs per configuration (repeatability), identical sensor chain everywhere
(odometry of \autoref{sec:odom}, same configuration files); ``no USBL'' = neither seed
nor factors (fixed-heading start), ``+ USBL'' = seed + back-end factors
$\sigma = 2.5$. \autoref{tab:chap1} gathers both configurations; this section reads
Bruce-SLAM alone, \autoref{sec:chap1-usbl} what the USBL anchor changes.

With its native loops only (SSM/NSSM), Bruce-SLAM already corrects the odometry by an
order of magnitude:
\begin{itemize}
\item from 19.6 m of raw drift down to \textbf{2.58--3.56 m} of start-pinned ATE,
  without degrading the heading (median 2.2--3.0°) — \autoref{fig:bruce-traj}
  and \autoref{fig:bruce-err};
\item per re-pinned section: 2.57 / 5.61 / 2.80 m (run 2,
  \autoref{fig:bruce-sections}) — S2 concentrates the drift, with few revisits to
  correct it;
\item the map is correct, not just sharp: point cloud at \textbf{0.09 m} (median)
  from the cloud re-rendered at the ground-truth poses, p90 0.70 m
  (\autoref{fig:bruce-map} and \autoref{fig:bruce-cloudgt}).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Adding the USBL anchor}
\label{sec:chap1-usbl}

The same system with the USBL anchor of \autoref{sec:usbl} (seed + back-end factors,
$\sigma = 2.5$) gives \autoref{fig:bruce-usbl-traj} to
\autoref{fig:bruce-usbl-sections}, and a start-pinned ATE of \textbf{8.09--10.90 m} —
\emph{worse} than Bruce alone. The mechanism is worth stating precisely, because it
motivates chapter~2:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{$^{1}$The USBL factors correct positions, not the frame heading.} The
  initial heading of these runs is estimated from the course-over-ground of the
  first USBL fixes (noisy: 10--22° off depending on the run), and nothing in this
  method corrects it afterwards (the factors only touch $x,y$). In the raw world
  frame, the whole trajectory stays rotated by the seed error — over a 90 m
  footprint, $\sim$1.6 m of ATE per degree. The same error shows in the relative
  metric: Trans.~\% takes $\sim$1~\%/degree of offset (measured: seed offset
  0.3° $\to$ 4.83~\%; 10.5° $\to$ 11.96~\%; 22.4° $\to$ 23.63~\%). Bruce alone
  starts from a fixed heading that happens to be close to the true one, hence its
  better numbers.
\item \textbf{What is missing is a heading correction} — the anchor is not at fault
  (the map metric is unchanged, 0.09 m median, and the position residuals are
  pulled to the USBL fixes), the \emph{frame} is. Correcting the heading requires
  revisits: this is exactly what the appearance-based loops of chapter~2 bring, and
  why the combination Sonar Context + USBL works where USBL alone does not.
\end{itemize}
